Managing Blood Sugar for a Healthier Life.

At its worst point, diabetes can lead to diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) or hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state (HHS). These are life-threatening conditions that result from extremely high blood sugar levels. Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA): This occurs primarily in people with type 1 diabetes but can also affect type 2 diabetics. When insulin levels are too low, the body starts breaking down fat for energy, producing ketones, which cause the blood to become too acidic. Symptoms include extreme thirst, frequent urination, nausea, vomiting, confusion, and fruity-scented breath. If untreated, it can lead to coma or death. Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS): More common in type 2 diabetes, HHS occurs when blood sugar levels rise extremely high, leading to severe dehydration. It can cause confusion, seizures, and eventually coma. In both cases, urgent medical attention is required to prevent serious complications or death.
